## Domain Context and Terminology

### Purpose and Goals
- **How would you describe the overall purpose of this problem statement in your daily work?**
  
    “In my day-to-day work, the core purpose of this problem is to make sure that citizens actually receive government services without unnecessary struggle.

    Currently, most problems arise not because services don’t exist, but because they are scattered across departments, handled differently by different offices, and heavily dependent on manual follow-ups.

    From an administrative point of view, this problem statement is about bringing order and consistency to service delivery. A unified platform helps both sides: citizens don’t have to visit multiple offices, and officers like us can track, verify, and process requests in a more structured and accountable way.

    So in simple terms, the purpose is to bridge the gap between citizens and government systems by creating a single, transparent channel through which services are requested, processed, and delivered.”

- **What are the primary goals or outcomes of this problem statement?**
  
  - Enable citizens to access all government services through a single platform
  - Reduce the need for physical visits to multiple government offices
  - Streamline inter-departmental coordination and workflows
  - Provide real-time tracking of application status
  - Establish clear timelines and accountability for service processing

## Core Workflows
Description of 3 real workflows as explained by the domain expert

### Workflow 1: Online Certificate Issuance (Income / Caste / Residence)
- **Trigger/Start condition:**  
  A citizen submits an online application for a certificate along with required documents.

- **Steps involved (in order):**  
  1. Application is received and checked for completeness.  
  2. If documents are missing or unclear, the application is returned to the citizen.  
  3. A Department Officer verifies the submitted details and documents.  
  4. The application is forwarded to the approving authority.  
  5. Approval or rejection decision is recorded.

- **Outcome / End condition:**  
  Certificate is issued digitally, or the application is rejected with a reason.

---

### Workflow 2: Welfare Scheme Application Processing
- **Trigger/Start condition:**  
  A citizen applies for a welfare scheme or financial benefit.

- **Steps involved (in order):**  
  1. Eligibility criteria are checked based on submitted information.  
  2. Documents are verified by the Department Officer.  
  3. Cross-verification may be done with other departments if required.  
  4. Application is reviewed by a supervisor for approval.  
  5. Final approval or rejection is communicated to the citizen.

- **Outcome / End condition:**  
  Welfare benefit is approved and processed, or the application is rejected.

---

### Workflow 3: Grievance Redressal Process
- **Trigger/Start condition:**  
  A citizen raises a grievance due to service delay, rejection, or lack of response.

- **Steps involved (in order):**  
  1. Grievance is registered and assigned to the concerned department.  
  2. Department reviews the issue and identifies the cause.  
  3. Corrective action is taken or clarification is provided.  
  4. If unresolved, the grievance is escalated to a higher authority.

- **Outcome / End condition:**  
  Grievance is resolved and closed, or escalated for further action.

## Rules, Constraints, and Exceptions
Document rules that govern how the domain operates.

- **Mandatory rules or policies:**  
  - Applications must follow defined eligibility criteria and document requirements.  
  - Services must be processed within prescribed timelines as per government norms.  
  - Officers must record decisions and reasons for approval or rejection.  

- **Constraints or limitations:**  
  - Dependence on multiple departments for verification and approvals.  
  - High workload during peak service periods.  
  - Partial digitization and manual intervention in some cases.  

- **Common exceptions or edge cases:**  
  - Missing or damaged documents due to genuine reasons.  
  - Mismatch of citizen data across departments.  
  - Special cases requiring manual review or supervisor approval.  

- **Situations where things usually go wrong:**  
  - Delays caused by inter-department coordination.  
  - Lack of clear communication to citizens about application status.  
  - Manual errors during document verification or data entry.

## Current Challenges and Pain Points

- **Most difficult or inefficient parts of the process:**  
  - Document verification is time-consuming and often requires manual checks.  
  - Coordinating between multiple departments slows down service processing.  
  - Citizens frequently require assistance due to lack of clarity in procedures.  

- **Where delays, errors, or misunderstandings usually occur:**  
  - During inter-department approvals and data validation.  
  - When documents are incomplete, outdated, or unclear.  
  - Due to manual data entry and inconsistent updates to application status.  

- **Information hardest to track or manage today:**  
  - Real-time status of applications across departments.  
  - Historical records of previous applications or benefits.  
  - Accountability details, such as identifying where and why delays occurred.

## Assumptions & Clarifications

- **Assumptions confirmed:**  
  - Citizens prefer a single platform to access multiple government services.  
  - Transparency in application status reduces follow-ups and grievances.  
  - Defined workflows and roles improve accountability in service delivery.  

- **Assumptions corrected:**  
  - Not all services can be fully automated; human intervention is still required.  
  - Uniform processes do not always apply due to policy and departmental variations.  
  - Digital platforms alone cannot address all citizen support needs.
